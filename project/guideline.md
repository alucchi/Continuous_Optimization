# In-class Presentation

All presentation dates are mandatory, even when you are not presenting. We will check your presence. 
When you are presenting, please bring your tablet or laptop. 

Due to the number of teams presenting and the time limit, keep your presentation within 15 minutes and prepare 5 min for Q&A. 
Other audience and we could ask you questions regarding the topic. 

## Expected Format
Introduction (motivation, goal ...) [~ 5 min]
Related Work [~5 min]
Methodology, results in progress [~5 min]
Q&A [~5 min]

## Grading Scheme

6.00: 
- Demonstrates mastery of the topic.
- Clearly explains the mathematical foundations.
- Provides novel insights.
- Critical analysis of results.
- Clear presentation structure with good time management.

5.00:
- Solid understanding of the topic.
- Contains some mathematical foundations.
- Provides ordinary insights. 
- Some critical analysis present but superficial.
- General audience-friendly presentation.

4.00:
- Bare understanding of the topic but not beyond.
- Little mathematical foundations.
- Insights more or less known in the field.
- Plain report of the results.
- Script reading or tedious slides.

3.00 - 1.00 (Fail):
- **Any clue of unploished AI generated slides, code or results ! See the counter examples.**
- No understanding of the topic.
- No mathematical foundation.
- No insights.
- No trustworthy results.
- Chaotic presentation or severely overtime.
